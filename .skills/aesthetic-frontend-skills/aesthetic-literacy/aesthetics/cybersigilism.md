**Palette**: Monochrome black and greyscale on skin/fabric with restrained chrome or icy metallic accents.

**Type**: Minimal or absent; sharp coded sigilic marks, black-metal-adjacent lettering, and techno-occult labels.

**Texture**: Tattoo linework, scarification illusion, embroidery, mirrored spikes, skin-tight prints, and biomechanical filigree.

**Shape**: Barbed curves, mirrored sigils, thorned anatomy paths, rib arcs, blade points, tribal hooks, and circuit traces.

**Motion**: Serpentine growth, contour reveals, activation-rune pulse, subtle body-scan shimmer, and no rave-neon dashboard motion.

**Spatial**: Body-centric and contour-following: skin, garment panels, intimate surfaces, and mirrored anatomical placement.

**Cultural markers**: Post-human beauty discourse, Y2K tribal revival, H.R. Giger, tattoo flash, occult sigils, nightlife scenes, and self-customization.

**Non-negotiables**: black linework + anatomy-hugging sigil geometry + post-human/biomechanical connotation + body-ornament logic. If these are removed, the result collapses into an adjacent or generic style rather than Cybersigilism.

## Connotation

**Source-backed contemporary characterization.** Treat the style as a multi-dimensional semiotic system and preserve the boundaries documented in the research packet.

## Scope

Acknowledge critique around decontextualized 'tribal' borrowing; keep focus on body-ornament logic rather than generic chrome cyberpunk.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from `cyberpunk` and `cybergoth`: Cybersigilism is monochrome anatomy-following tattoo/body ornament, not megacity neon or rave gear.
