**Type**: Theatrical display lettering, marquee or neon-outline logic, distance-readable capitals, game-label typography, retro Vegas signforms, and supporting serif luxury branding. Neutral sans can support UI copy, but sign-style type carries the aesthetic.

**Texture**: Neon glow, bulb-light simulation, green felt, glossy playing cards, plastic/clay chip surfaces, roulette lacquer, chrome/brass trim, dark reflective panels, velvet lounge cues, and polished poster gloss. Texture should mix glow, gloss, felt, and metal rather than flat paper alone.

**Shape**: Chips/discs, roulette circles, playing-card rectangles, suit icons, dice cubes, stars, arrows, marquee pylons, sign silhouettes, betting-grid zones, and stage-like frames. Geometry is symbolic and theatrical, not minimal.

**Motion**: Pulsing lights, blinking marquee suggestions, rolling dice, shuffling cards, stacking chips, roulette spin, jackpot bursts, and slow lounge shimmer. Motion should be subtle and reduced-motion friendly; avoid mandatory flashing or slot-machine spam.

**Spatial**: Two canonical modes: layered exterior-sign spectacle and ordered table-grid interiors. Layouts are dense, multi-zone, stage-like, and atmospheric, with foreground table objects, midground plaques or betting zones, and background neon/sign architecture.

**Cultural markers**: Chips, cards, dice, roulette, blackjack/craps/baccarat language, table felt, pit/table signage, marquee neon, jackpot/reward language, Vegas strip spectacle, matchbooks, coasters, showgirl or resort-theme references as supporting signals.
