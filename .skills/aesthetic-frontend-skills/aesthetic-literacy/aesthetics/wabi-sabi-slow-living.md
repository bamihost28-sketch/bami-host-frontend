**Palette**: Clay white, ash gray, soot black, moss, lichen, weathered brown, muted earth red, paper off-white, pale wood, and stone.

**Type**: Restrained serif or humanist sans, modest hierarchy, quiet scale shifts, and no brush-font or fake Zen calligraphy.

**Texture**: Unglazed stoneware, ash glaze, rough clay, patina, worn wood, bamboo, handmade paper, linen, visible repair, and stains.

**Shape**: Asymmetrical bowls, off-center vessels, low tables, natural-edge objects, handmade irregular silhouettes, and humble geometry.

**Motion**: Near-still fades, steam drift, breathing reveals, contemplative pauses, and reduced-motion-first interactions.

**Spatial**: Secluded, sparse, intimate, contemplative negative space, careful placement, and quiet incompletion.

**Cultural markers**: Chanoyu, Sen no Rikyū, wabi-cha, Raku/Shigaraki/Bizen bowls, impermanence, age, humility, care, and repair.

**Non-negotiables**: imperfection + impermanence + humble materiality + contemplative spatial restraint. If these are removed, the result collapses into an adjacent or generic style rather than Wabi-Sabi / Slow Living.

## Connotation

**Authentic philosophical lineage / careful contemporary translation.** Slow living is a modern adjacent layer, not the historical core of wabi-sabi.

## Scope

Do not flatten into beige wellness minimalism or fake Zen branding; keep tea culture, impermanence, humble materiality, and aging visible.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from `warm-minimalism`, `quiet-luxury`, and `scandinavian-modern`: weathering, irregularity, incompletion, and humble impermanence are required.
