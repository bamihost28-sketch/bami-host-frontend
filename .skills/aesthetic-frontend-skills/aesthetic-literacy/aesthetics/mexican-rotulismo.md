**Palette**: Sunny sign-paint brights anchored by wall neutrals: tomato red (`#E83B2E`), lemon yellow (`#FFD23F`), market green (`#2FA84F`), aqua (`#19A7A8`), cobalt (`#2467C5`), warm cream (`#F5E3B3`), and charcoal outline (`#24201C`). Colors are saturated and friendly, often outlined for readability against painted-wall or storefront surfaces.

**Type**: Hand-painted commercial lettering. Use rounded block caps, outlined display letters, shadowed words, casual scripts, and uneven but confident spacing. Digital implementations should use sign-painter display faces sparingly and pair them with a simple sans for body/utility text. Lettering must carry the identity; bright color alone is insufficient.

**Texture**: Painted wall and brush evidence. Subtle stucco/wall paint grain, brush drag, enamel sign wear, faded edges, and hand-filled color fields create tactility. Use sparing overlays and irregular strokes; avoid dirtying the whole interface into grunge.

**Shape**: Storefront communication shapes: cartouches, arched sign panels, speech-bubble labels, menu-board rectangles, scalloped badges, illustrated CTA blocks, and mascot/service icons. Corners are friendly (`8–18px`) but not generic app pills; outlines and drop shadows can mimic hand-painted dimensional lettering.

**Motion**: Friendly signboard movement. Short bounce or nudge (`160–260ms`), marquee flicker, mascot wink, and hand-painted wipe-ins are appropriate. Motion should feel like a shopkeeper calling attention, not casino animation.

**Spatial**: Dense but hospitable storefront stacking. Hierarchy follows sign logic: main offer/name first, pictorial explanation second, service/menu modules below. Use stacked panels, painted labels, image-plus-text pairing, and modular menu grids. Density is warm and legible, not chaotic.

**Cultural markers**: neighborhood storefront signs; hand-painted food and service illustrations; mascot characters; price/menu panels; outlined Spanish lettering; humor and friendliness; pictorial explanations for everyday commerce.

**Non-negotiables**: hand-painted typographic personality + friendly commercial tone + illustration and text feeling co-made + storefront/menu-board layout logic. If it becomes merely a bright "Latin" palette or ironic kitsch, it fails.

## Connotation

**Contemporary revival** — translate a living Mexican sign-painting tradition respectfully into UI. The tone should be warm, service-oriented, and locally commercial rather than gallery appropriation or tourist kitsch. Preserve the craft logic of lettering and pictorial communication.

## Scope

**Suitable for**: neighborhood food, market, local-service, cultural guide, event, and community-commerce experiences. **NOT suitable for**: sterile enterprise SaaS, quiet luxury, formal institutions, or any implementation that uses cultural markers as costume without business/signage logic.

**Related / Subsets**: Shares DIY energy with *punk-zine*, but rotulismo is welcoming and service-oriented rather than anti-authoritarian. Shares density with *maximalism*, but every element behaves like storefront communication. Shares texture with *grunge-typography*, but brush and wall evidence should remain warm and legible, not abrasive.
