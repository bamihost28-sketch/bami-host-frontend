**Palette**: Cheerfully saturated — bold greens (#00B32C, the unofficial Web 2.0 color), bright blues (#0099FF, #1E90FF), energetic oranges (#FF8C00), punchy pinks, and clean whites. Gradients simulate glossy "wet table" reflections from light to saturated mid-tones. Rarely uses dark backgrounds — optimism in color.

**Type**: Large, friendly sans-serif for trust and approachability — VAG Rounded, Arial Rounded, Trebuchet MS, Lucida Grande. Generous tracking, high x-height for readability, often white text on saturated gradient backgrounds. Informal and warm rather than corporate-authoritative.

**Texture**: Glossy "wet table" surfaces via `linear-gradient` reflections — a white-to-transparent stripe across the top 30% of buttons, simulating overhead light on curved glass. Starbursts, reflection strips, shiny badges. CSS: `background: linear-gradient(180deg, rgba(255,255,255,0.8) 0%, rgba(255,255,255,0) 50%)` over a saturated base; `::after` pseudo-elements for diagonal reflection bands and ribbon badges.

**Shape**: Aggressively rounded — `border-radius: 8–12px` on buttons, 15–20px on containers, pill shapes for tags. Large glossy CTA buttons with inner glow. "Beta" and "New!" badge/stamp shapes. Everything feels inflated, friendly, and non-threatening.

**Motion**: Slide-out panels, accordion reveals, star-rating animations. `transition` on hover for button-glow intensification. Smooth, approachable, never jarring. Drop-down menus with rounded corners and gentle transitions.

**Spatial**: Generous whitespace — content breathing room was a deliberate reaction to the dense, table-crammed layouts of Web 1.0. Large hero areas with centered content. Fixed-width layouts (960px standard) with ample padding. Content-first hierarchy with spacious navigation.

**Cultural markers**: Flickr, early Twitter (rounded tabs, pastel blue), Digg (bold blues + orange accents), Basecamp, early Facebook. Starburst badges ("Free Account!", "Sign Up Now!"), "Beta" tags as perpetual-trust signals, mascot characters (reddit alien, Digg mascot), informal copywriting ("Hey there!"), glossy reflection strips on footers.

**Non-negotiables**: glossy reflection gradient + rounded everything + cheerful saturated palette + beta/starburst badges

**Google Fonts**: `'Nunito'` (rounded, friendly, high x-height), `'Varela Round'` (compact rounded), `'Quicksand'` (geometric rounded, lightweight), `'Comfortaa'` (rounded geometric display). All share the rounded-terminal, approachable personality that defined the Web 2.0 era. Avoid sharp serifs, condensed faces, or anything that reads as corporate-authoritative.

**Connotation**: Nostalgic quotation — people deploying Web 2.0 Gloss today are quoting the 2004–2009 era with fondness. The "beta" badges, starbursts, and glossy reflections are deployed knowingly, with affection for early consumer-web optimism. It is rarely ironic (the aesthetic is too earnest to mock effectively); the nostalgia is warm, not cynical.

**Scope**: Suitable for startup landing pages, "beta" launch microsites, SaaS onboarding flows, community platform homepages, friendly consumer apps, and event registration pages. Excels when the brand voice is informal and approachable. NOT suitable for luxury brands (gloss reads as cheap, not premium), serious journalism, enterprise dashboards, healthcare interfaces, government services, or any context where visual restraint signals trustworthiness.

**Subsets / Related**: *Frutiger Aero* (existing entry) — shares gloss, gradients, and rounded corners but differs fundamentally in palette (nature/glass/swoosh vs cheery saturated), imagery (water droplets/bokeh vs badges/stars), and cultural tone (techno-optimistic futurism vs approachable consumer-web trust). Web 2.0 Gloss is about *consumer-web trust and friendliness*; Frutiger Aero is about *nature-meets-technology wonder*. They share CSS techniques but are distinct aesthetic systems. *Y2K* (existing entry) — predecessor; Web 2.0 Gloss swaps Y2K's futurism/chrome for friendliness/gloss.
