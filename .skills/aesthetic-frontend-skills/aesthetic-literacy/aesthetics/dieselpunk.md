**Palette**: muted olive, gunmetal, rust brown, cream, propaganda red, soot gray.

**Type**: deco sans, wartime stencil, condensed poster titling.

**Texture**: oil, soot, rivets, weathering, brushed steel, smoke.

**Shape**: armor plates, exhausts, tanks, treads, bomber noses.

**Motion**: rumble, march, propeller churn, piston cycling.

**Spatial**: hangars, trenches, war-torn cities, motor pools.

**Cultural markers**: interwar/WWII machine culture, pulp adventure, petroleum infrastructure.

**Non-negotiables**: muted olive, gunmetal, rust brown, cream, propaganda red, soot gray + oil, soot, rivets, weathering, brushed steel, smoke + armor plates, exhausts, tanks, treads, bomber noses + hangars, trenches, war-torn cities, motor pools.

## Connotation

Dieselpunk carries a specific robotics/speculative-technology connotation; use the source-backed cues above as a system rather than as isolated moodboard decoration.

## Scope

Use when the product, interface, or visual artifact benefits from this exact machine/body/worldbuilding language. Avoid when a neighboring aesthetic would communicate the intent with fewer cultural or accessibility risks.

## Related / Subsets

Related to atomic-age through retrofuturism, but older/heavier/oilier; related to steampunk, but combustion and war industry replace brass clockwork.
