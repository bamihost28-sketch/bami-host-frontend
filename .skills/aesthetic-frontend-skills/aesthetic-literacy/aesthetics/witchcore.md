**Palette**: Black, charcoal, forest green, plum, oxblood, amber candlelight, bone, dried herb greens, and tarnished brass (`#111111`, `#2A1F2D`, `#263B2A`, `#5B1E33`, `#C07A2D`, `#E8DEC8`). Color contrast should feel nocturnal but readable.

**Type**: Apothecary serif labels, engraved occult caps, small chart annotations, and readable sans support copy. Use high-contrast serif display sparingly, title case for ritual headings, and letterspaced caps for talismanic labels.

**Texture**: Smoke, wax, velvet, dried botanicals, parchment, etched star charts, ink bleed, candle glow, and glass jar translucency. CSS textures can use radial shadow pools, subtle noise, botanical line art, and amber glow.

**Shape**: Circles, crescent moons, pentacles, jars, talismans, altar trays, arched cards, and framed correspondences. Radii range from squared apothecary labels to soft jar/card corners (`6–16px`).

**Motion**: Candle flicker, smoke drift, slow reveal, lunar phase progressions, and orbiting celestial marks. Motion is patient (`500–1200ms`) and ritualistic, never bouncy SaaS delight.

**Spatial**: Altar/tabletop framing, cabinet-of-curiosity clustering, ritual grids, inventory drawers, moon-calendar rows, and central object focus. Dense symbolic clusters should still leave a clear primary action.

**Cultural markers**: Tarot, crystals, moon phases, spell bottles, ravens, herbs, candles, grimoires, apothecary labels, altar cloths, correspondences, protective symbols.

**Non-negotiables**: occult-symbol framing + ritual/apothecary object logic + dark natural palette with candlelight warmth + ceremonial pacing. If these are removed, the result becomes a generic adjacent style rather than Witchcore.

## Connotation

**Contemporary revival**. The signal is modern esoteric domesticity, not Halloween novelty; use symbols respectfully and avoid flattening living spiritual practices into decoration.

## Scope

Suitable for journaling, wellness-adjacent ritual tools, boutique storefronts, lunar trackers, inventory panes, and fantasy-adjacent editorial systems. Not suitable for clinical safety-critical health UX unless symbols are toned down.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from `dark-academia` because it is ritual and occult rather than institutional/bookish. Distinct from `cottagecore` because nature appears as herb, omen, and spell material rather than pastoral domestic comfort. Adjacent to Storybook Gothic but less heraldic and more altar/object oriented.
