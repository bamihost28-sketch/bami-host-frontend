**Palette**: black, gunmetal, nicotine amber, blood red, sodium yellow, cold white.

**Type**: severe condensed sans, police-terminal numerics, dossier labels.

**Texture**: wet reflection, smoke, ghosting, grain, scratched glass.

**Shape**: blinds, light slashes, interrogation rectangles, scanner windows.

**Motion**: slow scanlines, suspense reveals, rain drift, alarm pulses.

**Spatial**: isolated pools of light in hostile darkness; detective rooms/alleys.

**Cultural markers**: Blade Runner, Terminator, artificial persons, surveillance, fatalism.

**Non-negotiables**: black, gunmetal, nicotine amber, blood red, sodium yellow, cold white + wet reflection, smoke, ghosting, grain, scratched glass + blinds, light slashes, interrogation rectangles, scanner windows + isolated pools of light in hostile darkness; detective rooms/alleys.

## Connotation

Techno-noir carries a specific robotics/speculative-technology connotation; use the source-backed cues above as a system rather than as isolated moodboard decoration.

## Scope

Use when the product, interface, or visual artifact benefits from this exact machine/body/worldbuilding language. Avoid when a neighboring aesthetic would communicate the intent with fewer cultural or accessibility risks.

## Related / Subsets

Related to cyberpunk but narrower, slower, and more investigative; distinct from synthwave's constant neon glamour and HMI's operator clarity.
