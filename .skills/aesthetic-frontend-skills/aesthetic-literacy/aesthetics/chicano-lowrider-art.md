**Palette**: Candy red, turquoise, aqua, violet, black, chrome silver, pinstripe white, metallic gold, and jewel gradients.

**Type**: Hand-painted script, plaque lettering, mural/airbrush titles, magazine-cover display, neighborhood signage, and automotive/Chicano lettering.

**Texture**: Chrome, candy paint, clearcoat gloss, velvet upholstery, engraved metal, mural airbrush haze, and pinstriping.

**Shape**: Long low silhouettes, hydraulic stance, Impala curves, spoke/rim geometry, script flourishes, mural panels, and boulevard arcs.

**Motion**: Low-and-slow cruising, smooth glide, proud procession, hydraulic bounce/lift, and public reveal.

**Spatial**: Boulevard display, public gathering, show-and-tell parking, neighborhood procession, magazine spreads, and car-as-stage composition.

**Cultural markers**: Chicana/o identity, postwar customization, cruising, Lowrider Magazine, neighborhood pride, technological ingenuity, and Chicano movement connections.

**Non-negotiables**: low-and-slow motion + customized-car craft + Chicano community pride + glossy painted/chrome finish. If these are removed, the result collapses into an adjacent or generic style rather than Chicano Lowrider Art.

## Connotation

**Source-backed contemporary characterization.** Treat the style as a multi-dimensional semiotic system and preserve the boundaries documented in the research packet.

## Scope

Treat as living Chicano community expression; avoid criminalization, gang shorthand, police-light menace, or extracting chrome without social context.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from hot rod, muscle-car Americana, and generic retro car culture: lowrider art centers low-and-slow display, Chicano identity, craft pride, and community visibility.
