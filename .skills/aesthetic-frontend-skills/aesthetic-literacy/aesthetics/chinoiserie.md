**Palette**: Lacquer black, deep red, porcelain white, cobalt, celadon, jade, gilt, coral, peach, and Rococo pastels.

**Type**: Elegant serif display and restrained fine-detail labels; no generic brush-script or pseudo-calligraphy.

**Texture**: Lacquer gloss, porcelain sheen, gilding, painted wallpaper, silk, carved density, and screen-panel surfaces.

**Shape**: Asymmetrical cartouches, fret borders, pagodas, branches, cranes, rocks, parasols, porcelain vessels, and panels.

**Motion**: Slow ceremonial panel reveals, delicate parallax, branch/trellis drift, and curtain/screen transitions.

**Spatial**: Layered panels, inset scenes, ornamented borders, screen/cabinet logic, and aristocratic room envelopes.

**Cultural markers**: European fantasy of China/East Asia, export porcelain, lacquer, pagodas, bamboo/trellis, colonial trade, and exoticism critique.

**Non-negotiables**: lacquer/porcelain surface logic + asymmetrical ornamental framing + European exoticist connotation + elite decorative density. If these are removed, the result collapses into an adjacent or generic style rather than Chinoiserie.

## Connotation

**Historical quotation / critical revival.** Chinoiserie is a European decorative fantasy about China and East Asia, not an authentic Chinese design system.

## Scope

Use as European chinoiserie or critical quotation, not as authentic Chinese design; avoid fake calligraphy, pan-Asian motif soup, and racialized exotic fantasy.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from `rococo`, japonisme, guochao, and authentic Chinese visual traditions; it is European fantasy ornament rooted in trade, exoticism, and decorative invention.
