# References — Aesthetic Literacy

## Theoretical Foundations

- Barthes, R. (1957). *Mythologies*. Éditions du Seuil. → Semiotic reading of popular cultural forms as myth-systems; foundational for understanding aesthetic movements as sign systems, not merely visual preferences.

- Bourdieu, P. (1984). *Distinction: A Social Critique of the Judgement of Taste*. Harvard University Press. → The social logic of aesthetic taste; essential for understanding why aesthetics function as cultural signals and class markers.

- Baudrillard, J. (1981). *Simulacra and Simulation*. Éditions Galilée. → Relevant to internet-native aesthetics (vaporwave, early internet) and the simulation/pastiche dynamic in contemporary digital aesthetic.

## Design History

- Meggs, P. B., & Purvis, A. W. (2016). *Meggs' History of Graphic Design* (6th ed.). Wiley. → Reference for historical movements: Bauhaus, Swiss International Style, Art Nouveau, Art Deco, Memphis/Post-Modern.

- Woodham, J. M. (1997). *Twentieth-Century Design*. Oxford University Press. → Broad design history covering modernism through postmodernism and the material culture of industrial design aesthetics.

## Digital Culture & Internet Aesthetics

- Lialina, O., & Espenschied, D. (Eds.). (2009). *Digital Folklore*. Merz & Solitude. → The vernacular visual culture of early internet; formative for early internet / Web 1.0 aesthetic and glitch art.

- Lialina, O. (2012). "Prof. Dr. Style." *Rhizome*. → Short essay on amateur web aesthetics; relevant to early internet aesthetic family.

- Gibson, J. J. (1979). *The Ecological Approach to Visual Perception*. Houghton Mifflin. → Affordance theory; underpins how design language communicates usability through material metaphors.

## Contemporary Aesthetic Culture

- Chayka, K. (2020). *The Longing for Less: Living with Minimalism*. Bloomsbury. → Cultural analysis of minimalism's appeal; relevant to quiet luxury and clean-girl aesthetics.

- Le Zotte, J. (2023). *From Goodwill to Grunge: A History of Secondhand Styles and Alternative Economies*. University of North Carolina Press. → Thrift and vintage aesthetics as cultural economy; useful context for cottagecore and dark academia.

## Online Aesthetic Lexicons

- **Aesthetics Wiki** — [https://aesthetics.fandom.com/wiki/Aesthetics_Wiki](https://aesthetics.fandom.com/wiki/Aesthetics_Wiki) — Community-maintained encyclopedia of named aesthetics; use as a supplementary reference for lesser-known movements, not as a primary authority.

- **Are.na** — [https://www.are.na/](https://www.are.na/) — Curated visual research boards; useful for cross-referencing aesthetic imagery.

## Fatigue & Trend Analysis

- Goman, D. (2022). "Corporate Memphis is Everywhere. Why?" *Eye on Design / AIGA*. → Analysis of Corporate Memphis fatigue; reference for applying the fatigue-risk flag to that aesthetic.
