**Palette**: Nicotine beige, faded mustard, moss green, smoke gray, washed brown, institutional cream, oxidized teal, and dusk black.

**Type**: Mid-century educational sans, modest serif captions, library-music sleeve type, schoolbook labels, and broadcast-era kerning.

**Texture**: Paper aging, tape hiss as grain, vinyl crackle, photocopy softness, film dust, print fade, halftone, and scanlines.

**Shape**: Sober rectangles, caption boxes, modest circles, diagrams, map markers, institutional frames, and title-card layouts.

**Motion**: Slow drift, loop, dissolve, flicker, projector jitter, tape wobble, and ghosted reveal.

**Spatial**: Archival title cards, isolated ephemera, educational panels, booklet/page spreads, and public-information sequencing.

**Cultural markers**: BBC Radiophonic Workshop, Ghost Box, library music, children’s television idents, public-information films, analog decay, and lost futures.

**Non-negotiables**: degraded archival texture + institutional/educational design language + subdued palette + lost-futures cultural signal. If these are removed, the result collapses into an adjacent or generic style rather than Hauntology.

## Connotation

**Nostalgic quotation shading into spectral critique.** Hauntology is temporal dislocation and media-memory residue, not generic spooky vintage.

## Scope

Avoid lazy spooky-retro reductions; preserve lost-futures and public-media memory.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from `vaporwave`, `early-internet`, and `liminal-space-backrooms`: it is analog-cultural spectrality and public-media residue, not consumer simulation or vacant architecture.
