**Palette**: Premium Chinese heritage palette: lacquer red (`#B5121B`), vermilion (`#E43D30`), imperial gold (`#C99A2E`), jade (`#1E8A66`), ink black (`#15110D`), porcelain white (`#F7F1E5`), and deep indigo (`#203A5F`). Color should be ceremonial and disciplined, not a generic red/gold flood.

**Type**: Script-aware hierarchy. Use refined serif or premium sans for body/product information, with calligraphic, seal-script, brush-script, or vertical-label accents reserved for titles, stamps, and brand moments. Chinese typographic identity must be structural: vertical labels, seal marks, bilingual balance, and script-sensitive spacing matter more than decorative font swapping.

**Texture**: Premium material references: embossed foil, paper/fabric fibers, ink-wash gradients, lacquer sheen, carved seal impressions, and subtle brocade or cloud-pattern accents. Texture is selective and ceremonial; avoid all-over ornament that destroys product focus.

**Shape**: Framed heritage geometry: medallions, seals, fans, arches, layered borders, moon-gate/card frames, centered product shrines, and balanced symmetry. Corners may be squared or softly rounded (`4–12px`), but frames should feel intentional and crafted.

**Motion**: Restrained ceremonial motion. Use scroll/processional reveals (`350–700ms`), seal-stamp impacts (`180–260ms`), foil glints, and slow parallax of layered patterns. Motion should feel luxurious and deliberate, never meme-like or hyperactive.

**Spatial**: Premium commerce and story-panel structure. Center-weighted heroes, generous negative space around products, framed story sections, dense ornamental accent zones, and disciplined product grids. Heritage detail should support scanning, not bury it.

**Cultural markers**: contemporary Chinese brand confidence; seals and stamps; calligraphy/brush accents; festival and gift packaging; jade/red/gold symbolism; opera, court, folk, cloud, wave, and paper-cut motifs used with restraint; boxed-set product ceremony.

**Non-negotiables**: integrated contemporary Chinese heritage references + ceremonial premium product framing + typography carrying Chinese identity + selective structured ornament. Red/gold decoration alone is not Guochao.

## Connotation

**Contemporary revival** — present-tense cultural confidence filtered through premium retail and culture-tech branding. It should not read as museum nostalgia, orientalist fantasy, or ironic pastiche. The strongest implementations combine modern product clarity with heritage symbolism that feels designed into the system.

## Scope

**Suitable for**: premium retail, tea/cosmetics/collectibles, cultural institutions with contemporary branding, festival campaigns, product launches, and heritage-aware commerce. **NOT suitable for**: generic international SaaS, contexts that cannot support script-aware typography, or implementations that reduce Chinese identity to dragons and red/gold ornament.

**Related / Subsets**: Differs from *maximalism* because ornament is structured and ceremonial, not abundance for its own sake. Differs from *art-deco* because the luxury grammar is Chinese heritage and contemporary commerce, not interwar Euro-American geometry. Differs from *city-pop*, which is retro Japanese leisure nostalgia rather than current Chinese identity branding.
