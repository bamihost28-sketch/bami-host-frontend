**Palette**: Lagoon turquoise, ocean teal, bamboo brown, torch amber, coral, tropical green, sunset orange, rum mahogany, and bar black.

**Type**: Hand-painted hospitality signs, carved menu display, postcard script accents, and mid-century menu typography; no pseudo-tribal novelty fonts.

**Texture**: Bamboo, rattan, tapa-like patterning, weathered wood, ceramic mug gloss, rope, shell, matting, condensation, and smoky darkness.

**Shape**: Mug silhouettes, A-frames, hut geometry, lanterns, barware, shield/diamond motifs, and mid-century clustered garnishes.

**Motion**: Slow sway, rain/storm ambience, torch flicker, cocktail-theater reveals, and lava-lamp bubbling.

**Spatial**: Immersive low-light lounge, prop-dense escapist enclosure, nooks, hanging objects, and room-as-themed set.

**Cultural markers**: Don the Beachcomber, mid-century American faux-Polynesia, rum cocktails, exotica music, Hollywood restaurant sets, and appropriation critique.

**Non-negotiables**: immersive themed hospitality + mid-century American pop packaging + tropical barware/ephemera + explicit cultural caution. If these are removed, the result collapses into an adjacent or generic style rather than Tiki / Polynesian Pop.

## Connotation

**Historical quotation with critique.** This is American Polynesian Pop / tiki bar Americana, not authentic Pasifika design.

## Scope

Frame as mid-century American Polynesian Pop / tiki bar Americana, not Indigenous Pasifika design; avoid sacred symbols, faux-tribal fonts, and hula caricature.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from actual Polynesian/Pasifika design and from cleaner mid-century modern: it is American themed-hospitality fantasy with appropriation critique built in.
