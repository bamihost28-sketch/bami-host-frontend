**Palette**: Genre-led palettes: fantasy jewels, Euro-strategy prestige colors, bright family-game schemes, sci-fi cools, or boutique minimal variants.

**Type**: Large title as brand object, genre-coded custom logotype, subordinate subtitles/awards, and thumbnail-readable hierarchy.

**Texture**: Polished illustration, composited paint, print-ready finish, atmospheric fog, parchment, brushed material, cosmic glow, or indie collage.

**Shape**: Square box face, central tableau/emblem, title block, icon clusters, character groups, map/emblem insets, and frame devices.

**Motion**: Frozen narrative promise, setup/unboxing suspense, resource cycling, charged poses, and implied play experience.

**Spatial**: Foreground focal hook plus background world context; shelf-distance and thumbnail readability; promise of internal system.

**Cultural markers**: Theme telegraphy, publisher sheen, award laurels, player-count/age cues, designer/franchise cues, and tabletop worldbuilding promise.

**Non-negotiables**: shelf-read title hierarchy + instant thematic legibility + worldbuilding promise + polished packaging finish. If these are removed, the result collapses into an adjacent or generic style rather than Board Game Box Art.

## Connotation

**Commercial packaging meta-aesthetic.** It is not a single movement; it is the cover-art regime for tabletop products and their subgenres.

## Scope

Use the source-backed boundary notes; do not reduce the aesthetic to palette alone or flatten adjacent traditions into a generic moodboard.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from `trading-card-game-design` and pure fantasy illustration: it is shelf-distance packaging that promises an internal tabletop system.
