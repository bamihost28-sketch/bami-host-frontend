**Palette**: Office blues/greys, invoice whites, muted toolbar navy, red/green exception states, fuel-price yellow, and spreadsheet grey (`#F8FAFC`, `#1E3A8A`, `#64748B`, `#DC2626`, `#16A34A`, `#FACC15`).

**Type**: Utilitarian sans, spreadsheet numerals, category abbreviations, toolbar labels, compact form copy, and all-caps status chips. Use tabular figures for totals, inventory, and reconciliation.

**Texture**: Gridlines, report tables, input-heavy forms, accounting density, toolbar chrome, row striping, and printable-report surfaces. Texture is ledger/operations repetition.

**Shape**: Square widgets, toolbar rows, modal reports, multi-column data blocks, exception badges, and spreadsheet-like cells. Corners are low (`0–6px`) and forms are compact.

**Motion**: Posting confirmations, batch actions, exception alerts, dropdown edits, row validation, and minimal animation. Motion should be nearly invisible and operational.

**Spatial**: Dashboard-ledger hybrid, low decorative whitespace, dense lists/forms, persistent side navigation, and module groupings by shift, fuel, inventory, vendors, and accounting.

**Cultural markers**: Price book, tank reconciliation, shift closeout, vendor invoices, promo windows, lottery/tobacco categories, shrink reporting, cash drawer totals, fuel inventory.

**Non-negotiables**: operator-facing retail density + price/inventory/reconciliation object model + form-and-ledger texture + minimal decorative motion. If these are removed, the result becomes a generic adjacent style rather than Convenience-Store Back-Office Grid.

## Connotation

**Authentic operational backoffice**. It should feel like store infrastructure, not consumer ecommerce or generic enterprise SaaS polish.

## Scope

Suitable for price books, shift closeouts, fuel operations, inventory, vendor reports, and multi-store admin. Not suitable for shopper-facing retail experiences.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from generic ERP because c-store objects such as fuel tanks, promo windows, and shift balancing are load-bearing. Distinct from `high-performance-hmi`: retail/accounting operations rather than process telemetry.
