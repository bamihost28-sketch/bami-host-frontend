**Palette**: Monochrome photography plus one or two ink accents: deep blue, black, off-white, muted red, teal, or ochre (`#0B1F3A`, `#111111`, `#F2EBDD`, `#B23A30`, `#2F7F86`). The palette should feel printed and restrained.

**Type**: Condensed and extended sans, uppercase rhythm, asymmetrical line breaks, oversized artist/title relationships, and typographic syncopation. Use strong weight contrast and deliberate line spacing like album sleeve composition.

**Texture**: Photo grain, halftone, overprint feel, matte sleeve tactility, ink trapping, and offset-print imperfection. Avoid glossy music-app gradients.

**Shape**: Rectangular crops, large text blocks, color bars, typographic columns, photo windows, and strict sleeve rectangles. Corners are usually square; rhythm comes from crop and type placement.

**Motion**: Sparse fades, crop reveals, title slides, record-sleeve wipes, and needle-drop transitions. Motion should be cool and editorial (`240–500ms`) rather than kinetic club graphics.

**Spatial**: Frontal album-cover composition, generous negative space, portrait plus type field, asymmetrical balance, and modular sleeve grids that feel composed rather than template-driven.

**Cultural markers**: Francis Wolff-style musician photography, Reid Miles typography, liner-note poise, jazz seriousness, session dates, catalog numbers, vinyl sleeve artifacts, instrument silhouettes.

**Non-negotiables**: monochrome photo plus limited accent color + syncopated typographic composition + album-cover frontal structure + cool editorial restraint. If these are removed, the result becomes a generic adjacent style rather than Blue Note Jazz Modernism.

## Connotation

**Contemporary revival**. It carries mid-century jazz credibility and intellectual cool; keep it respectful and avoid generic retro lounge clichés.

## Scope

Suitable for archives, music editorial, listening rooms, cultural institutions, and premium content grids. Not suitable for highly colorful pop campaigns or generic audio player chrome.

**Related / Subsets**: Related to `swiss-international` through modernist type discipline, but distinct through musician portrait crops, sleeve pacing, and jazz-specific cultural signals. Less universalist than `bauhaus`; less commercial-pop than `city-pop`.
