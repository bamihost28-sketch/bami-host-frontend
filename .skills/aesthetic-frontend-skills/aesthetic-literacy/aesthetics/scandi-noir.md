**Palette**: Charcoal, slate blue, cold gray, winter white, desaturated teal, sodium amber, black water, dirty concrete, and blood-dark red.

**Type**: Restrained sans-serif, public-institution clarity, subtitle/signage sobriety, case-file labels, and transport-signage dryness.

**Texture**: Wet asphalt, wool, fog, sleet, concrete, glass condensation, coarse knit, low-light grain, puddles, frost, and matte interiors.

**Shape**: Utilitarian geometry, harsh horizontals, bridges, apartment blocks, institutional interiors, corridors, docks, shorelines, and file rectangles.

**Motion**: Deliberate procedural pacing, slow pursuit, windshield glide, weather drift, surveillance gaze, and patient reveal.

**Spatial**: Weather-separated figures, emotional-distance interiors, sparse exteriors, transport/bridge/harbor liminality, and horizon-heavy emptiness.

**Cultural markers**: Welfare-state tension, flawed detectives, wintry/damp settings, border/bridge spaces, social critique, humanized criminality, and municipal realism.

**Non-negotiables**: cold desaturated climate palette + low-key realism + institutional typography + social-critique noir layer. If these are removed, the result collapses into an adjacent or generic style rather than Scandi Noir.

## Connotation

**Contemporary regional crime idiom.** Darkness may be literal night or bleak daylight; the noir half matters as much as the Scandinavian half.

## Scope

Use the source-backed boundary notes; do not reduce the aesthetic to palette alone or flatten adjacent traditions into a generic moodboard.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from `scandinavian-modern`, classic noir, and `techno-noir`: it pairs Nordic climate/place with institutional realism, social critique, and low-key procedural tension.
