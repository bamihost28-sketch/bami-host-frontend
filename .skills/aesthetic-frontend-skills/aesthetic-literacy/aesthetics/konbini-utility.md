**Palette**: Shelf whites, chain stripe accents, warm food photography, receipt black, service blue/green/red, and private-label cream (`#FFFFFF`, `#F5F2EA`, `#E60012`, `#005BAC`, `#008C45`, `#111827`). Use repeated accent bands rather than lifestyle gradients.

**Type**: Pragmatic sans with large Japanese/Latin support, clear numerics, compact labels, and packaging-style hierarchy. Headings are friendly but direct; microcopy must fit shelf tags and checkout modules.

**Texture**: Matte packaging board, shelf-label repetition, small utility icons, product photo cutouts, receipt paper, barcode texture, and laminated service signage.

**Shape**: Onigiri triangles, circular product photos, rounded barcode/service chips, superellipse frames, shelf strips, coupon tabs, and kiosk tiles. Radii are practical (`6–18px`).

**Motion**: Scanner flashes, basket additions, queue-safe confirmations, stepwise service flows, and receipt printouts. Motion should be quick, transactional, and reassuring (`120–260ms`).

**Spatial**: Compressed but legible modules, shelf-as-grid logic, transactional signposting, product/service lanes, and high information density with clear wayfinding.

**Cultural markers**: Onigiri opening diagrams, private-brand bands, coffee cups, bento labels, service kiosks, self-checkout signage, barcode/receipt cues, payment icons, seasonal shelf callouts.

**Non-negotiables**: service/packaging utility over lifestyle polish + convenience-store wayfinding logic + compact low-friction information density + private-label visual systems. If these are removed, the result becomes a generic adjacent style rather than Konbini Utility.

## Connotation

**Authentic or contemporary revival**. It should feel like convenient everyday infrastructure, not exoticized Japanese decoration; utility is the cultural marker.

## Scope

Suitable for checkout, quick baskets, food pickup, kiosk flows, store maps, loyalty coupons, and compact commerce interfaces. Not suitable for luxury or sparse editorial experiences.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from `city-pop`, which is nostalgic music/urban leisure. Distinct from `warm-minimalism`, which is calmer and more spacious; Konbini Utility is operational and shelf-driven.
