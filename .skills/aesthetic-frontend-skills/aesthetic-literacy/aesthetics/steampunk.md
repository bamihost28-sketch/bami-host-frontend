**Palette**: brass, copper, sepia, dark brown, soot black, aged ivory.

**Type**: Victorian serif, engraved labels, cabinet typography.

**Texture**: gears, patina, turned wood, leather, gauges, boiler soot.

**Shape**: automatons, boiler torsos, monocular eyes, pistons, clockwork arms.

**Motion**: tick, wind, hinge, crank, piston, pressure release.

**Spatial**: workshops, libraries, airships, laboratories, Victorian streets.

**Cultural markers**: Verne/Wells, automatons, inventor culture, steam power.

**Non-negotiables**: brass, copper, sepia, dark brown, soot black, aged ivory + gears, patina, turned wood, leather, gauges, boiler soot + automatons, boiler torsos, monocular eyes, pistons, clockwork arms + workshops, libraries, airships, laboratories, Victorian streets.

## Connotation

Steampunk carries a specific robotics/speculative-technology connotation; use the source-backed cues above as a system rather than as isolated moodboard decoration.

## Scope

Use when the product, interface, or visual artifact benefits from this exact machine/body/worldbuilding language. Avoid when a neighboring aesthetic would communicate the intent with fewer cultural or accessibility risks.

## Related / Subsets

Related to dieselpunk as retro-industrial speculation but earlier, warmer, and more ornate; related to arts-and-crafts through material craft.
