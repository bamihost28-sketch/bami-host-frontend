**Palette**: Cream (#f5f0e1), black (#1a1a1a), white (#ffffff), gold (#c5a44e), muted olive (#7a8b5e), deep burgundy (#5c1a1f), silver (#c4c4c4)

**Type**: Proto-geometric sans-serif lettering; regimented spacing; integrated typographic logos (Moser's WW mark); tall condensed display capitals

**Texture**: Smooth plaster walls; gilded flat surfaces; bentwood grain; polished marble inlay; burnished metal fixtures

**Shape**: Rectilinear geometry — square-grid proportions, orthogonal lines, cubic massing; no organic whiplash curves; the rectangle is the primary form

**Motion**: Deliberate, architectural — forms arrive in measured sequence; no kinetic or flowing movement; stillness as authority

**Spatial**: Austere, ordered, modular — rooms composed as total works (Gesamtkunstwerk) with nothing extraneous; each object occupies its own rectangular plane

**Cultural markers**: Hoffmann's square-grid obsession; Moser's integrated logo marks; Olbrich's gilded Secession dome; Klimt's geometric-gold phase; the Sitzmaschine chair's honest function-disclosure

**Non-negotiables**: rectilinear geometry + square-grid discipline + cream/black/gold palette

## Connotation
**Mode: authentic.** The Vienna Secession occupies a unique position: it is simultaneously a historical period style (1897–1914) and an unbroken lineage. The Secession's rectilinear geometry, square-grid thinking, and Gesamtkunstwerk philosophy flow directly into Bauhaus and then into modern corporate and institutional design. When a contemporary architecture firm uses black/cream/gold and a square-grid layout, they are not "quoting" Vienna 1900 — they are working in a tradition the Secession inaugurated. The aesthetic carries authority and institutional weight, never nostalgia or irony.

## Scope
**Suitable:** Architecture firm and design studio sites, luxury brand identities (fashion, jewelry, hospitality), cultural institutions (museums, galleries, opera houses), editorial and publishing platforms aiming for gravitas, high-end real estate, law firms and professional services wanting understated power. The Secession's language of grid-based rectilinear confidence projects institutional permanence.

**NOT suitable:** Playful or casual brands, tech startups (reads as too formal), children's products, food trucks and casual dining, entertainment/media, any brand that wants to seem "approachable" or "friendly." The Secession's austerity is its strength — and its limitation. It cannot do warmth, humor, or informality without breaking its own rules.
