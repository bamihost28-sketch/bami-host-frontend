**Palette**: Pastel grounds + bold primaries + black-and-white pattern; deliberately non-harmonious

**Type**: Bold slab serif or decorative sans; used decoratively rather than purely readably

**Texture**: Squiggle pattern, polka dot, zebra stripe — pattern as noise

**Shape**: Funky geometry — triangles meeting circles; squiggles as structural elements

**Motion**: Playful and unpredictable; random energy

**Spatial**: Pattern fills all space; no "breathing room" philosophy

**Cultural markers**: Squiggly line, Sottsass lamp, bold geometric clashing pattern, 80s product design

**Non-negotiables**: pastel + bold pattern mixing + anti-harmonious palette

**Connotation**: Postmodern play and graphic irreverence; witty, youthful, and decorative rather than timeless or austere.

## Robotics / Speculative-Tech Notes

Memphis Design robotics is best handled as a product/object branch of Memphis, anchored by Masanori Umeda's Ginza Robot cabinet and toy-like postmodern object language. Translate it with clashing segmented color, laminate-like pattern, playful antennae, ball-wheel bases, sticker labels, and anti-functional humor.
