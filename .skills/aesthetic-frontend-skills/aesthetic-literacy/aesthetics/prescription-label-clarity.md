**Palette**: White label fields, amber/orange bottle references, black text, alert red/yellow, patient identity color rings, and soft clinical grey (`#FFFFFF`, `#F59E0B`, `#111827`, `#DC2626`, `#FDE047`, `#E5E7EB`).

**Type**: Large readable sans for drug names and instructions, strong warning hierarchy, tabular dates/dosage counts, and plain-language body copy. Minimum text sizes should exceed generic app defaults.

**Texture**: Plastic-plus-paper contrast, removable cards, tactile rings, label adhesive edges, icon stamps, and matte pharmacy stock. Texture is tangible safety architecture.

**Shape**: Bottle/cap reading planes, stacked label fields, color rings, warning wedges, dosage cards, and same-view grouping blocks. Corners are modest and functional (`2–8px`).

**Motion**: Reveal-on-rotate logic, sequential dosage displays, refill-state transitions, warning expansion, and strong state changes. Motion must be predictable and safety-preserving.

**Spatial**: Same-field-of-view grouping, top-loaded hierarchy, breathing room around critical data, repeated patient/medication/dosage zones, and warning areas that never compete with decoration.

**Cultural markers**: Warning icons, ownership color rings, dosage cards, cap-top reading orientation, pharmacy labels, refill dates, accessibility aids, instruction-first wording.

**Non-negotiables**: oversized medication/instruction hierarchy + same-view grouping of critical info + warning-zone clarity + tactile label architecture. If these are removed, the result becomes a generic adjacent style rather than Prescription Label Clarity.

## Connotation

**Authentic patient-safety utility**. It should feel calm, domestic, and trustworthy; do not aestheticize medication or hide warnings behind style.

## Scope

Suitable for medication managers, refill systems, patient portals, caregiver dashboards, and instruction cards. Not suitable for generic health marketing pages without dosage-specific information architecture.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from `high-performance-hmi`: domestic medication safety rather than industrial operations. Distinct from `flat-design` and `material-design`: label/dosage hierarchy is the aesthetic engine, not generic simplicity.
