**Palette**: Candy-bright rainbow stacks: bubblegum pink (`#FF6EC7`), aqua (`#42D9D4`), lemon (`#FFE65A`), lilac (`#B78CFF`), mint (`#7EF2A0`), cherry red (`#FF3B5C`), plastic white (`#FFF7FB`), and occasional pastel variants. Use a clean neutral anchor for utility copy so joyful density remains readable.

**Type**: Bubbly rounded sans and sticker-caption display type. Headings can use rounded, inflated, or cutout-style letterforms; labels may use mixed-script kawaii caption energy. Body and settings text need a cleaner rounded sans. Type should feel like part of a sticker/accessory system, not a generic cute theme.

**Texture**: Toy-plastic and craft accessory tactility: glossy charms, foam stickers, puffy vinyl, bead shine, felt/fleece softness, scrapbook decals, and translucent acrylic clips. Implement with small gloss highlights, layered sticker shadows, and material-specific badges rather than one global gradient.

**Shape**: Accessory stacking vocabulary: hearts, stars, flowers, bows, blobs, clips, beads, rounded tabs, sticker frames, charm badges, and over-decorated edge clusters. Corners are soft (`14–28px`), but the key shape behavior is layering and accumulation around content.

**Motion**: Toy-like microinteractions. Use charm bounce, sticker pop, confetti bursts, bead wiggle, inventory pickup, and badge attach motions. Timing is quick and elastic (`180–360ms`, spring/back easing), but reduced-motion must preserve the static accessory signal.

**Spatial**: Cheerful anti-minimalist crowding with a readable center. Decoration clusters along edges and corners while core cards, forms, and navigation remain discoverable. Use layered callouts, sticker piles, friend/profile cards, charm inventories, and dense collectible grids with clear content islands.

**Cultural markers**: Harajuku street-snap energy; hair clips and bead bracelets; kawaii characters; DIY accessory overload; rainbow plastic charms; sticker-book/social-profile layouts; playful self-styling and community expression.

**Non-negotiables**: visible accessory stacking + toy/plastic material cues + intentional playful clutter around readable modules + joyful self-styling tone. Bright colors without layered accessories are not decora-kei.

## Connotation

**Authentic / nostalgic quotation** — exuberant self-styling, youth-community expression, and Harajuku fashion lineage. It should feel sincere and joyful, not cynical "kawaii overload." The interface can be highly decorated, but it must protect a center of gravity for actual use.

## Scope

**Suitable for**: social profiles, creator/fan communities, sticker-book tools, toy/collectible commerce, playful onboarding, youth-culture editorial, and virtual wardrobe/inventory products. **NOT suitable for**: serious enterprise, financial/medical workflows, quiet luxury, or text-heavy knowledge work.

**Related / Subsets**: Related to *maximalism* through abundance, but decora-kei's abundance specifically comes from wearable accessory stacking. Related to *y2k* through bright plastic and youth culture, but not chrome futurism. Related to *memphis* through playful shapes, but decora-kei is character/accessory-led rather than postmodern abstract geometry.
