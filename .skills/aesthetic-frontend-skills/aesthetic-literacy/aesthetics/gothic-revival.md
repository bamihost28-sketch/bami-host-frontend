**Palette**: Dark wood, oxblood, forest green, ink black, stone gray, parchment, brass, amber, and stained-glass jewels.

**Type**: Blackletter in small doses, engraved serif body, heraldic caps, inscriptional titling, and readable Victorian support type.

**Texture**: Carved wood, leaded glass, stone, wrought iron, encaustic tile, embossed leather, velvet, and parchment.

**Shape**: Pointed arches, lancets, trefoils, quatrefoils, tracery, finials, spires, vergeboards, and shrine frames.

**Motion**: Solemn upward reveals, candle flicker, stained-glass light shifts, processional fades, and no jump-scares.

**Spatial**: Vertical framing, chapel/library alcoves, room-as-shrine, picturesque asymmetry, and ordered ornament.

**Cultural markers**: Medievalism, stained glass, heraldry, church windows, chivalric romance, Pugin/Willement/Morris lineages, and craft revival.

**Non-negotiables**: pointed-arch/tracery form language + material gravity + medievalist revival connotation + craft/piety undertone. If these are removed, the result collapses into an adjacent or generic style rather than Gothic Revival.
