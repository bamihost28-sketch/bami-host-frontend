**Palette**: High-saturation retail-counter brights: flame red/orange/yellow, electric blue/cyan, lime, magenta, purple, hot pink, and premium metallic gold/silver (`#E1111A`, `#FF7A00`, `#FFD400`, `#00AEEF`, `#6B2DFF`, `#FF2DAA`, `#C0C0C0`, `#111827`). Prize fields use aggressive black/white keylines and retail burst seals rather than casino-lounge darkness.

**Type**: Oversized compressed block display sans, cartoon/script variants, thick black outlines, bevel/inline highlights, drop shadows, prize-first headline hierarchy, compact instructional microcopy, tabular serials, and legal/footer text. Headlines shout the top prize; small copy explains “winning numbers,” “your numbers,” bonus play, odds, validation box rules, void marker logic, and no-cash-value status.

**Texture**: Printed-paper substrate with paper grain, print misregistration, halftone bursts, guilloche/security print, silver/gold foil simulation, currency swirls, gem/chrome shine, serial/barcode noise, and matte grey/silver latex scratch coating. Scratched states should leave residue, dust, ragged chipped edges, and debris flakes along the brush path; texture should feel like a disposable state-issued artifact, not a screen-native casino game.

**Shape**: Tall rectangular ticket frames, repeated reveal circles/medallions, dense play grid compartments, dotted separators, row labels, prize labels, bonus spots, badge bursts, denomination stamps, price badge and lottery badge lockups, barcode strips, validation boxes, crossword/puzzle modules, and laddered bonus compartments. Corners can be sharp or lightly rounded (`0–6px`) but the object should remain ticket-like.

**Motion**: Reveal-first physical motion: drag, scrape, uncover, match, scan, bonus, multiply. Digital motion should reveal only the current brush path on pointer drag, support partial/messy states, require substantial coverage before completion, and provide accessible keyboard/button fallbacks; a single click must never reveal the full ticket.

**Spatial**: Mini-poster at the top plus mini-form below: denomination/price badge, lottery badge or specimen badge, oversized title, top-prize callout, instruction microcopy, prize legend, winning-number row, your-number grid, bonus cells, prize boxes, serial, barcode/validation box, void marker, and legal/footer strip. Density is high, compartmentalized, and retail-readable with low whitespace.

**Cultural markers**: Scratch zones, grey/silver latex, “winning numbers,” “your numbers,” multipliers, top-prize bursts, dollar signs, cash stacks, coins, vaults, barcodes, serials, validation text, specimen marks, fictional/non-redeemable disclaimers, state-lottery product framing, and convenience-counter optimism.

**Non-negotiables**: drag-based reveal-ticket object model + scratch/security-print texture with grey/silver latex residue and debris + prize-first dense play grid hierarchy + price/lottery/serial/validation ephemera + convenience-retail/state-lottery context. If these are removed, the result becomes a generic promo flyer or drifts into casino.

## Connotation

**Nostalgic quotation or authentic retail ephemera mode**. It quotes the optimism and tactile density of scratch-off tickets while avoiding real-money gambling UX. Preserve clearly fictional, specimen, non-redeemable, no-cash-value, bragging-rights-only copy. Do not let it drift into casino glam, table-game objects, sportsbook odds, or generic coupon/flyer design.

## Scope

Suitable for demo scratch-card interfaces, gradual drag scratch reveals, instant-win-inspired reveal moments, prize-grid layouts, ticket-like promotion artifacts, and state-lottery ephemera studies. Not suitable for casino interiors, table games, sportsbook odds boards, or luxury nightlife branding.

Crossword scratchers, bingo scratchers, and premium foil tickets are internal modes, not separate slugs in this pass.
