**Palette**: Black and white photocopy base with naloxone orange/red, clinic blue, emergency yellow, and marker green (`#111111`, `#FFFFFF`, `#E95D2A`, `#2563EB`, `#FDE047`, `#16A34A`). Color marks urgency and navigation, not decoration.

**Type**: Heavy serif/sans headings, clear body copy, pamphlet hierarchy, handwritten annotations, and bilingual-friendly layouts. Use large scannable section titles and plain-language instruction text.

**Texture**: Photocopy grain, risograph misregistration, folds, stamps, marker circles, tape, cut-and-paste diagrams, and pocket-zine tactility. Texture should support trust and peer distribution.

**Shape**: Panels, checklists, warning boxes, illustrated steps, folded-page pagination, sticker callouts, dosage cards, and resource strips. Shapes must frame action and safety.

**Motion**: Accordion reveals, sequential instruction steps, emergency mode expansion, low-friction utility transitions. Motion is restrained and should never obscure urgent content.

**Spatial**: Compact but legible modules, poster-to-pamphlet adaptability, repeatable steps, strong left-to-right/top-to-bottom scan, and high contrast between warnings and resources.

**Cultural markers**: Naloxone cards, safer-use explainers, bilingual health sheets, peer-distributed resources, mutual-aid language, hotline/resource strips, street-medic practicality.

**Non-negotiables**: instructional clarity + mutual-aid/peer-care tone + DIY print tactility + warning-and-checklist hierarchy. If these are removed, the result becomes a generic adjacent style rather than Harm Reduction Zine.

## Connotation

**Authentic community-care utility**. This aesthetic requires ethical restraint: do not aestheticize crisis, obscure instructions, or turn harm-reduction work into edgy decoration.

## Scope

Suitable for resource hubs, emergency guides, outreach tools, public-health explainers, and community dashboards. Not suitable for decorative campaigns detached from care or safety.

**Related / Subsets**: Distinct from `punk-zine`: it shares DIY print texture but prioritizes peer-care clarity over anti-design aggression. Distinct from `constructivism`: bottom-up mutual aid, not top-down mobilization.
